# Lesson 62: Managing Calendars, Reminders, and Tasks with OpenClaw

Calendar management is not about filling every slot. It is about turning commitments into reliable reminders and traceable tasks.

## Skills Used

Search [skills.lc](https://skills.lc/) for `calendar`, `reminder`, `todo`, or `task`. In this environment, use `gog` for Google Calendar and Gmail, `apple-reminders` for Apple Reminders, `things-mac` for Things 3, and `trello` for team boards.

## Workflow

Extract actionable items first: owner, action, deadline, dependency, and definition of done. “Follow up next week” is not enough.

Choose the right destination. Fixed-time events go to calendars. Work items go to task tools. Collaborative work goes to a board. Recurring checks become automations.

Add source context to every task: meeting name, email subject, link, or decision record.

## Human Review

Changing someone else’s calendar, sending invitations, deleting tasks, or moving deadlines should require confirmation.

## Exercise

Give OpenClaw meeting notes and ask it to create calendar events, reminders, and a recurring follow-up task.

A good system does not remind you that you are busy. It prevents commitments from disappearing.
