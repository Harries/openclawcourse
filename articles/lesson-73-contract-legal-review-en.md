# Lesson 73: Contract and Legal Triage with OpenClaw

Contract review is high risk. OpenClaw can summarize clauses and flag issues, but it cannot replace legal advice.

## Skills Used

Search [skills.lc](https://skills.lc/) for `legal`, `contract`, or `document review`. In this environment, use `documents` for document review, `pdf:pdf` for PDFs, `spreadsheets` for risk tables, and `browser` for public references.

## Workflow

Identify the contract type first: procurement, services, SaaS, employment, NDA, channel, or license.

Extract key clauses: parties, amount, term, delivery, acceptance, payment, breach, confidentiality, IP, termination, and dispute resolution.

Create a risk list: missing clauses, unbalanced liability, vague delivery terms, excessive penalties, auto-renewal, and data-compliance issues.

End with questions for the business owner or lawyer.

## Human Review

OpenClaw does not provide legal advice. Signing, editing, or sending contract language requires authorized human review.

## Exercise

Give OpenClaw a SaaS service agreement and ask for a clause summary, risk table, and lawyer question list.

Legal triage helps experts see the important parts faster.
